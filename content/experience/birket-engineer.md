I worked at Birket Engineering from 2020 to 2023 as an Embedded Systems Engineer, leading embedded software and electrical architecture for interactive entertainment systems that demanded low latency, reliability, and maintainability in live environments.

## Disney's Galactic Starcruiser

For Disney's Galactic Starcruiser, I led the software and electrical architecture for interactive, low-latency props used in a live entertainment setting. The work included engineering custom PCBs, developing embedded firmware libraries in C/C++, and designing radio protocols that could synchronize show props during performance without introducing visible lag or instability. I also developed sensor fusion algorithms that combined IMU data with audio signals to drive dynamic effects, and the overall project contributed directly to more than seven figures in revenue with a 25% to 30% profit margin.

## Universal Studios Japan: Dream Coaster

On Universal Studios Japan: Dream Coaster, I led feature-driven and test-driven firmware development in C/C++ for a low-latency control system built around synchronized show behavior. I architected a real-time communication protocol to orchestrate animations across the experience, developed embedded LED driver algorithms for dynamic high-fidelity lighting effects, and implemented diagnostics and maintainability frameworks that helped operations teams reduce downtime. The delivered system was scalable, low maintenance, and helped increase company revenue by 20%.

## ICON Park Media Server

For the ICON Park Media Server project, I architected a real-time media server capable of handling up to 30 concurrent cabin audio streams. I also developed the companion iOS application and designed the low-latency audio network architecture that tied the system together. The project was delivered four weeks ahead of schedule, and the interoperability approach I implemented reduced integration costs during deployment.
